About
The game tackles about various disinformation techniques that can cause major problem in the community. Disinformation, also known as "fake news," is a growing problem in today's society. It refers to the deliberate spread of false or misleading information with the intention of manipulating public opinion and causing confusion. The rise of the internet and social media has made it easier than ever for disinformation to spread quickly and reach a large audience.

Disinformation can take many forms, from fabricated news stories and conspiracy theories to manipulated images and videos. It can be spread through various mediums such as social media, traditional news outlets, and even through word of mouth. The consequences of disinformation can be severe, from undermining trust in democratic institutions to inciting violence and spreading hate.

The spread of disinformation can have serious consequences, both in terms of short-term confusion and long-term harm to society. For example, false information about COVID-19 can lead to people not taking appropriate precautions or trusting in ineffective treatments. Disinformation can also be used to interfere in elections, by spreading false or misleading information about candidates or voting procedures. This can undermine the integrity of democratic processes and erode public trust in government.

Combating disinformation requires a multifaceted approach. One important aspect is fact-checking, which involves verifying information and identifying false or misleading content. Media literacy education is also crucial, as it can help people develop the skills to critically evaluate the information they encounter online. Another important aspect is regulation, which can involve taking steps to hold social media platforms and other companies accountable for the spread of disinformation on their platforms. Furthermore, international cooperation is vital as disinformation does not respect borders and often originates from one country and spread to another.

Disinformation is a serious problem that can have significant negative impacts on society. It is important to be aware of its existence and to take steps to combat it through fact-checking, media literacy education, and regulation.
Project
The group has decided to develop a web-application game called "Disinfo Quest" to educate and raise awareness about disinformation. The aim of the game is to educate players on how to spot and combat disinformation through interactive gameplay and real-world scenarios. Players will learn how to critically evaluate the information they come across and make informed decisions in the future. By playing "Disinfo Quest," players will become more aware of disinformation and better equipped to protect themselves and their communities from its harmful effects.

A game-based learning was implemented as an emerging technology to the group's web application. It was designed to help users develop digital literacy, critical thinking skills, and media literacy. It does so by using gaming elements and mechanics to make learning more interactive and engaging. The web application is for personalised-users, it means that the game does not connect the user to another user and is solely a program or a game for only one person to play.

The group is excited to offer "Disinfo Quest" as a web-application game that can be accessed from any device with an internet connection. It's designed to be easy to use and accessible for players of all ages and backgrounds. Join the group in the fight against disinformation by visiting the group's website and playing "Disinfo Quest" today. Learn how to spot and combat disinformation in your daily life, and become a more informed and engaged citizen in the digital age.
